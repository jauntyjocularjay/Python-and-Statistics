# Data Relation

## Bivariate Data

data that compares two variables and their relationship.

### Scatter Plot

plots data on cartesian coordinate plane

### Linear Relationships

#### regression line

- A regression line is a single straight line closest to all points
- `y = mx + b`
- `m` = slope, or `regression coefficient`
- allow prediction between directly related data points

#### Correlation

1. Measures the way two variables move together, both the direction and closeness of change
1. positive correlation indicated when two variables change in the same direction.
      - positive slope, positive regression coefficient
1. negative correlation occurs when to variables move in the opposite direction
      - negative slope, negative regression coefficient

#### Standard Deviations

1. used to scale correlation so it is always between `-1` and `1`.
1. aka correlation coefficient (`r`)
      - `+r` indicates positive regression coefficient (regression line slope)
      - `-r` indicates negative regression coefficient (regression line slope)

#### Squared Correlation (variance)

1. `r**2` is always between 0 and 1, tells us (as a float) how much variance in one variable is predicted by the other variable.
1. can be thought of as how accurate the prediction is if used with a linear regression
      - ex. `r**2 = 0.7` predicts a 70% variation in its outcome
      - ex. `r**2 = 1.0` predicts a 100% variation in its outcome

### Spurrious Correlations

are correlations that suggest a causal relationship where none exists.
